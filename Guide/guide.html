Pre-hardmode - How to Start terraria.

When you start off the game, you obviously have choice in difficulty. Journey, Classic, Expert, and Master (Journey is almost creative, classic is the basic game play with zero changes, Expert adds exclusive drops, Master is hardest with the same drops and statues for each boss.) I personally suggest classic, journey mode is a bit like playing with cheats and classic is pretty easy on its own.

Once you've made your character and world, it is finally time to start. First thing to do is to collect wood and make basic housing for your guide (the guide instantly spawns when a world spawns. Talk to him to see possible reciepes for items!). A basic house calls for a chair, door/way to leave, a table, solid block walls, and a background. Once you've made some housing, go mining for some gold/platinum (changes per world). These bars can be used to make really strong armor and accesories for any class. Any other weapons you find in chests that are class specfic can be useful (weapons such as finch staff and leather whip both found in chests).

Hardmode - What to do

Bosses

For bosses (this isnt much help but;) just keep fighting them, learn how they fight, their moves and what sized arena you need. Get the strongest equipment avaliable (my weapons list is pretty accurate for weapon usage, experiment with armor). Bosses are the most interesting part of terraria, and fighting your first boss is the most fun, I will not ruin that for anyone reading this.

Arena Making

Arena making is what helps most players beat certain bosses. An arena consists of many things such as, sunflowers(increase speed), Heart Laterns (increase health regen), campfires (more health regen), and the main material which is what you walk on is platforms (platforms are made from wood and can be passed through by press down or holding up.).

When you want to make an arena (specfically underground), clear a large area (a little larger than what can fit on your screen; roughly 175 blocks wide, and around 120 blocks tall) then place equally spaced out platforms (around 5 blocks apart). Craft campfires now with a couple torches to increase healing. Place these campfires so that you have the buff from the campfires anywhere in the arena. This is technically the minimum for an arena, just light it up with torches on the walls/platforms and tada! You are now ready to fight bosses. You can add sunflowers or heart laterns in addtion to campfires.

Classes Breakdown - What do the three Main Classes do?

Mages specialize in using mana which is a unit on the right side of the screen increased by getting mana crystals from fallen stars, which are found during night time. Mana can be regained from fallen enemies or by standing still. Mages use weapons such as Tomes (books), staffs, or other magic weapons like leaf gun (silly little leaf blower).

Warriors are the easiest class to play (they also got some new powerful weapons since the labour of love update). Warriors use flails, yo-yo's, spears, and swords. The warrior class armors have a very high defence stat, this is to conpensate them having to get close to hit the enemy.

Summoners are the most complicated class, but still a bit straight forward. Summoners use staffs and whips ONLY. Heres the catch, the staffs summon other things to fight for you and whips increase damage from those summons on certain enemies you hit. Summoners specilize is the art of doing nothing a sitting back and using a whip. Summoners have the worst defence for one reasons. They have some of the strongest choices for weapons.

Rangers are pretty straight forward, using ammo like bullets, arrows, or missles to fire powerful weapons. Rangers use pistols, rifles, bows, repeaters, and throwables like shurikens and knives.
